Question: 1. What is correlation in statistics and how is it measured?\nAnswer: Correlation in statistics is a measure that expresses the extent to which two variables are linearly related. It is a statistical tool used to describe simple relationships between variables without making a statement about cause and effect. Correlation coefficients range from -1 to 1, with 0 indicating no linear relationship, 1 indicating a perfect positive linear relationship, and -1 indicating a perfect negative linear relationship.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 2. How does correlation differ from causation? Provide an example to illustrate this difference.\nAnswer: Correlation is a statistical measure that expresses the extent to which two variables are linearly related, while causation refers to the relationship between cause and effect, indicating that one variable directly influences the other. 

An example to illustrate the difference between correlation and causation is the relationship between ice cream sales and shark attacks. There is a strong correlation between the two variables, meaning that as ice cream sales increase, so do shark attacks. However, this does not mean that eating ice cream causes shark attacks. In reality, the correlation between these two variables is likely due to a third variable, such as hot weather, which influences both ice cream sales and shark attacks independently. This example highlights that correlation does not imply causation.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 4. Discuss the range of correlation coefficients and what each value indicates in terms of the relationship between variables.\nAnswer: The range of correlation coefficients is from -1 to 1. Here is what each value indicates in terms of the relationship between variables:

1. A correlation coefficient of 1 indicates a perfect positive linear relationship between the two variables. This means that as one variable increases, the other variable also increases proportionally.

2. A correlation coefficient of -1 indicates a perfect negative linear relationship between the two variables. This means that as one variable increases, the other variable decreases proportionally.

3. A correlation coefficient of 0 indicates no linear relationship between the two variables. This means that there is no predictable pattern in the relationship between the variables.

In summary, the correlation coefficient helps to quantify the strength and direction of the relationship between two variables, with values closer to 1 or -1 indicating a stronger relationship, and values closer to 0 indicating a weaker or no relationship.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 10.
